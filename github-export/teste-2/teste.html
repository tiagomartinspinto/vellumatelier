Title:
Listening to Kuperkeikka: Podcasting, Place and Youth Voice in Eastern Helsinki


Abstract:
This article examines Kuperkeikka, a participatory podcast project developed with youth in Eastern Helsinki, Finland, as a form of place-based art education in an urban context. Rather than treating “place” as a neutral setting, the article approaches Eastern Helsinki as shaped by migration, segregation, schooling, and unequal educational opportunity. From this perspective, young people use podcasting as a creative and educational practice which they reflect on school, language, belonging, and exclusion in relation to the places they inhabit.
Drawing on arts-based research and critical pedagogy, the article analyses selected podcast transcripts alongside reflection on the project’s creative process. It asks how listening, dialogue, and storytelling can support young people in expressing local knowledge and lived experience in a diverse urban environment. A key dimension of the project was its multilingual form. Guests were invited to speak not only in English but also in their own language or languages, and some conversations took place in languages other than English. Episodes were transcribed in English, Finnish, and Portuguese, making translation and linguistic difference part of the project’s method.
The study is also informed by my positionality as an artist-educator, podcast maker, immigrant, and doctoral researcher working in the same context. During the project, while I was learning Finnish, many participating students were also negotiating language, schooling, and belonging. The article suggests that participatory podcasting can help young people speak, reflect, and make meaning across languages in urban educational contexts.
